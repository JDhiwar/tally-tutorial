Financial Year

GST

Full Form of GST - Goods & Service Tax

The tax levied by the government on any type of goods or services is called GST.

Nature of Business

Business For Purchase & Sales Of Goods - In which any kind of product is bought or sold to earn profit. Like electronics shop in which fridge a TV is bought and sold.

Business For Service Providing - In which any kind of service is exchanged. In which no goods are bought or sold. In which service is given and profit is earned. As mobile recharge, Item Repairing, Hotel Services, Restaurants etc.

Who are Compulsory For GST Registration

According to the rules of the government, those who sell more than 40 lakh products in a year, it is necessary to get them registered in GST.

Those who provide services of more than 20 lakhs in a year, it is necessary to get them registered in GST.

Document Required For GST Registration

1. Aadhar Card of Applicant

2. Pan Card of Applicant

3. Passport Size Photo of Applicant

4. Bank Account Front Page of Applicant

5. Valid Mobile Number of Applicant

6. Proof of Business Address

7. Rent Aggreement (if shop on Rent)

8. Firm Name

Apply GST Registration gst.gov.in

After applying, a number is received which is called GSTIN Number.

GSTIN Number

GSTIN Number Full Form - GST Identification Number

GSTIN Number consists of 15 digits.

First 2 Digit - State code

Next 10 Digit - Pan card Number of Applicant

Next 1 Digit - Entity Number of PAN Holder in a state

Next 1 Digit - By Default Z

Last 1 Digit - Random Number

Types of GST Rates

GST @ 00% - Items or services on which no GST is charged (Fertilizer, Seeds, milk, Eggs)

GST @ 05% - Items or services on which 5% GST is charged (Suger, Spices, Tea)

GST @ 12% - Items or services on which 12% GST is charged (water bottle, ghee, pickles)

GST @ 18% - Items or services on which 18% GST is charged (mobile, computers)

GST @ 28% - Items or services on which 28% GST is charged (Air Conditioners, luxury items, cement)

How GST Apply in Invoice

1. SGST (State Tax) & CGST (Central Tax) - Whenever an item is bought from the same city or sold in the same city, 50-50% of the tax & revenue tax is applicable

Example - If a mobile is bought in Chhattisgarh itself and sold it in any city of Chhattisgarh, then the tax will be as follows.

Mobile Value - 1000 Rs.
SGST 9% - 90 Rs.
CGST 9% - 90 Rs.
Total - 1180 Rs.

2. IGST (Interstate Tax) - IGST is applied whenever an item is bought or sold from another state.

Mobile Value - 1000 Rs.
IGST 18% - 180 Rs.
Total - 1180 Rs.

Composition Firm and Regular Firms

The firm is registered in two ways in GST.

1. Composition Firm

2. Regular Firm

Difference between the two firms

Sno. Subject Composition Firms Regular Firms
1 Turnover Limit If the firm's 1 year sales is less than 1.5 crores, then the firm can be registered in the composition. If a firm's sales of 1 year is more than 1.5 crores, then it is mandatory to register the firm in regular.
2 GST Return The composition firm has to make quarterly returns. Which is called CMP08 Return. The regular firm has to make 1 return every month which is called GSTR3B Return.
3 Applicable of Tax Rates In the composition firm, the input of GST on purchases cannot be taken nor can GST on sales be levied. Regular firms have to keep complete records of input GST and output GST.
4 Tax Liability The composition firm sells in every 3 months, it has to pay tax of 1% of the sale along with the return. If there is a tax payable on the accounting of GSTR3B return in a regular firm, then tax has to be paid, if it is not made then it is not to be done.
5 Example of Entry No SGST & CGST or IGST is levied on the entry of purchases or sales in the tally in the composition firm. On entering purchases or sales in Tally in a regular firm, the taxable value of the item is written off separately and Tax SGST & CGST Or IGST applicable thereon will be levied.
6 Name of Invoice The bill generated in the composition firm is called bill of supply. The bill generated in a regular firm is called a Tax Invoice.

Voucher

Voucher means a paper on which something is written. If I am doing business, I am also buying goods, staying in the middle, buying goods on credit and also selling on credit. I am taking payment from someone and giving payment to someone.To keep track of all these transactions, separate registers are made, so these registers are called vouchers.

Types of Vouchers

There are many types of transactions in a business viz.

Purchase of Goods
Sale of Goods
Payment
Receipt

1. Purchase Voucher(F9) - The purchase is noted in the purchase voucher.

2. Sales Voucher(F8) - Sales vouchers are used to enter sales.

3. Payment Voucher(F5) - Payment voucher is used to make payment to someone.

4. Receipt Voucher(F6) - On receipt of money from someone in the receipt voucher, it is entered.

5. Contra Voucher (F4) - There are three types of entry in this voucher. Cash to Bank, Bank to Cash and Bank to Bank. That is, depositing cash in the bank, withdrawing cash from the bank and depositing money from your own bank in any other bank.

6. Journal Voucher(F7) - Any type of entry can be made in the journal voucher, purchase, sale, payment or receipt. But to enter it, it is necessary to come to Dr. or Cr.

7. Debit Note / Purchase Return (CTRL + F9) - When the purchased goods are returned, their entry is made in the debit note.

8. Credit Note / Sales Return (CTRL + F8) - If the goods sold by us are returned, then its entry is made in the Credit note.

Bank Account - Bank account means such an account in which we deposit and withdraw money, in which we can do any transaction at any time.

Difference between savings account and current account.

Saving Account Current Account
Savings account is in the name of any individual person. Whereas the current account is in the name of the firm.
There is a limit for depositing money in the savings account. Income tax notice can come for depositing more money than savings. You can deposit any amount of money in the current account in the bank, according to your business transactions.
The passbook is given by the bank in the savings account. Whereas in the current account, the statement is given instead of the passbook.
Interest is earned on the money deposited in the savings account. There is no interest on the money deposited in the current account.

How to make Ladger of any bank.
